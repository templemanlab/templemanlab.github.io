    Principal Investigator

    Tier 2 Canada Research Chair in Cell Biology

    Michael Smith Health Research BC Scholar

    Dr. Nicole Templeman joined the Biology Department at the University of Victoria in 2020. She became fascinated in aging biology through her Ph.D. research at the University of British Columbia, where she studied how insulin levels affect metabolic health and lifespan in mice. Dr. Templeman expanded her exploration of age-related biological changes during her postdoctoral research fellowship at Princeton University. There, she focused on signaling pathways that regulate reproductive aging, using the nematode C. elegans as a model.   • Faria Athar, M.Res.

    Ph.D. candidate

    Faria Athar's research interests lie in understanding how nutrition regulates aging and age-associated diseases. Previously she studied at University College London, where she worked in the David Gems lab on dietary restriction and lifespan-extending mechanisms in C. elegans. Outside of the lab, Faria enjoys listening to podcasts, experimenting with food, and watching documentaries.

  • Muskan Karmani, B.Sc. Hons.

    M.Sc. student

    Muskan Karmani's research interests lie in understanding disease manifestations, particularly those involving hormone dysregulation. Muskan's undergraduate Honor's project examined the effects of various substrates on the growth of oyster mushrooms, which prompted her curiosity in learning more about how dietary modifications and nutrient intake can impact an organism's quality of life. This experience sparked her interest in studying the relationship between diet and health, specifically in the context of aging. Outside the lab, Muskan enjoys reading, weightlifting, and rewatching sitcoms.

  • Artemis Najafi, Ph.D.

    Postdoctoral Research Fellow

    WHRI Graduate and Fellowship Research Award in Women's Health

    Dr. Artemis Najafi is a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Templeman lab. Her research focuses on how insulin signaling influences ovarian and uterine aging, with the goal of identifying strategies to preserve reproductive health. Her broader interests include women's reproductive physiology and pathology, as well as the molecular and genomic mechanisms underlying reproductive aging, diseases, and disorders. Outside of the lab, she enjoys cooking new foods, painting, and camping.
